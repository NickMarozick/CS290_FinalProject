Essentials to Bring:

The Ten Essentials The must-haves for safety, survival, and basic comfort:
  1. Navigation
    • Map (with protective case)
    • Compass
    • GPS (optional)
  2. Clothing
    • Boots
    • Proper Attire
    • Clothese for the elements
  3. Light Source
    • Headlamp or flashlight
    • Extra batteries
  4. First Aid
    • Kit
  5. Fire
    • Matches or lighter
  6. Tools
    • Knife or multitool
  7. Food
    • Extra day's supply of food
  8. Water
    • Water bottle or Canteen
    • Water filter or other water treatment system
  9. Tent
  10. Sun protection
    • Sunscreen

Getting in Shape for the Trail and Rapids

The best way to train for backpacking is to mimic the activity as closely as possible. The amount of conditioning you need depends on your current fitness level and the kinds of trips you have planned. Start with day hikes and progress towards your goal, adding pack and expected weight as your conditioning improves. It's best to build up your chops and get your boots worn in before heading out for a challenging excursion. Similarly, in training for kayaking and rafting, it is best to replicate as closely as possible the activity. Some weight training, gym rowing machines, and kayaking in increasingly tough conditions to build muscular strength and cardiovascular endurance and recovery is recommended.
